<h1 class="titulo">Muralism</h1>
<h2 class="subtitulo">Community, collaborative and territorial practices</h2>

<figure class="imagen-con-caption">
  <img src="/assets/img/muralismo/muralismo-hero.jpg" alt="Community mural process record" loading="eager">
  <figcaption>Community muralism: process and result documented in a neighborhood context.</figcaption>
</figure>

<p class="parrafo" style="margin-top: 6%;">
  Within <strong>Community</strong>, the <strong>Muralism</strong> series gathers experiences in which mural practice becomes communal, relational, or participatory. It is not a single format or a fully uniform body of work: it includes facilitation, collective guidance, small-scale interventions, festivals, open-air museums, and transversal experiences.
</p>

<p class="parrafo">
  For that reason, its structure should first be read through <strong>working contexts and modalities</strong>, and only afterwards through individual work capsules. The main archive folders do not automatically correspond to finished artworks, but to frameworks where concrete works, records, processes, and supporting materials appear.
</p>

<p class="parrafo">
  This preserves the actual logic of community practice: the work does not always emerge in isolation, but within shared situations of production, listening, participation, and collective construction.
</p>

<h3 id="facilitation" class="subtitulo2">01. Facilitation and collective guidance</h3>
<p class="parrafo">
  This line gathers projects where the main role is not only to execute an image, but to accompany collective processes: mediation, participatory design, shared imagination building, and collaborative technical resolution.
</p>

<h3 id="interventions" class="subtitulo2">02. Light interventions</h3>
<p class="parrafo">
  These works involve smaller-scale or faster actions, where mural production adapts to the specific conditions of place, available time, and the needs of each context.
</p>

<h3 id="festivals" class="subtitulo2">03. Festivals</h3>
<p class="parrafo">
  Festivals appear here as intensive frameworks where mural production is linked to exchange among artists, public visibility, and the temporary activation of urban space.
</p>

<h3 id="openair" class="subtitulo2">04. Open-air museums</h3>
<p class="parrafo">
  In this modality, muralism is organized as a territorial constellation of works. The focus is no longer just on a single wall, but on the relationship between facades, routes, neighborhood life, and local memory.
</p>

<h3 id="overview" class="subtitulo2">05. Transversal overview</h3>
<p class="parrafo">
  This overview reads the series as an open field: different techniques, imaginaries, scales, and years coexist under the same community-oriented approach. Rather than closing the definition, it reveals the breadth of mural work within your archive.
</p>
