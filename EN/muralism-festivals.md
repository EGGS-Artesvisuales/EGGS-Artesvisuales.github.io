<h1 class="titulo">Muralism</h1>
<h2 class="subtitulo">Festivals</h2>

<figure class="imagen-con-caption">
  <img src="/assets/img/muralismo/fest/fest-amk-la-legua-2010.webp" alt="Mural made during Festival La Legua 2010" loading="eager">
  <figcaption>Festival La Legua, 2010. Web-optimized record from the FEST archive.</figcaption>
</figure>

<p class="parrafo" style="margin-top: 6%;">
  <strong>FEST</strong> gathers participations in festivals, encounters, challenges, meetings, and collective platforms for muralism, graffiti, and urban art. Each main folder corresponds to an authorial or crew participation within a specific event frame.
</p>

<p class="parrafo">
  In these cases, the production context is decisive: limited time, coexistence with other artists, immediate public visibility, circulation of techniques, and shared production. The work does not depend on a single formal language, but on its insertion into a collective instance.
</p>

<p class="parrafo">
  FEST differs from Open-air Museums and Facilitation because its entry criterion is participation in an event frame, not a fixed locality or a continuous community process. Even so, each capsule can connect with other branches of the archive through technique, period, collaborations, or territory.
</p>
