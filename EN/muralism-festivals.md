<h1 class="titulo">Muralism</h1>
<h2 class="subtitulo">Festivals</h2>

<figure class="imagen-con-caption">
  <img src="/assets/img/muralismo/muralismo-festival.jpg" alt="Festival mural panel" loading="eager">
  <figcaption>Participation in a mural festival and collective production in public space.</figcaption>
</figure>

<p class="parrafo" style="margin-top: 6%;">
  Festivals bring together a specific type of mural work: limited time, immediate public visibility, coexistence with other artists, and a strong component of exchange. In this frame, the work develops in front of others, in dialogue with the event and within an intensive temporality.
</p>

<p class="parrafo">
  This subseries gathers records in which the mural is produced as part of gatherings, celebrations, or open platforms, and where the work acquires an additional relational dimension: sharing techniques, occupying the space alongside other proposals, and activating the territory through simultaneity.
</p>

<p class="parrafo">
  More than a simple exhibition context, the festival operates here as a production system that modifies rhythm, reception, and the way the image enters circulation.
</p>
