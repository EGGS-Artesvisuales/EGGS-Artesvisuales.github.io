<h1 class="titulo">The Invisible</h1>
<h2 class="subtitulo">Invisible saturation as artistic matter</h2>

<figure class="imagen-con-caption imagen-con-caption-70">
  <img src="/assets/img/lo-invisible-pintura-wifi-04.jpg" alt="Wi-Fi Painting No. 4 from The Invisible series" loading="lazy">
  <figcaption>Representation of the World / The Tangible / Saturation / The Invisible / Wi-Fi Paintings, No. 4. Oil on panel, EGGS, 2020.</figcaption>
</figure>

<p class="parrafo">
  <strong>The Invisible</strong> reflects on the hidden side of saturation: electromagnetic fields, wireless networks, thermal variations, chemical emissions, ambient noise, and information overload. These forces are physical and real, but they cannot be seen directly.
</p>

<p class="parrafo">
  In this line of work, painting behaves like a slow sensor. The image becomes distorted, compressed, interrupted, or bent, as if the visible surface were affected by signals moving through the air. The work does not illustrate the invisible; it makes its effects perceptible.
</p>
