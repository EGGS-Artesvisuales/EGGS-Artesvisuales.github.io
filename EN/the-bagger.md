<section class="parrafo">
  <p>In <strong>El Empaquetador</strong>, the artist joins the work of a supermarket bagger for
  approximately two hours. A sticker is attached to every plastic bag handed out, making visible
  and countable an action that normally disappears inside everyday consumption.</p>
  <p>The action connected informal work paid through tips, accelerated consumption, and the
  multiplication of packaging. More than 400 stickers and over CLP $6,000 in tips recorded the
  economic and material intensity of that short shift.</p>
</section>

<h3 class="subtitulo2">CONCEPTUAL READING</h3>
<section class="parrafo"><p>The sticker turns every delivery into a visible unit. The bagger's body
is also gradually covered, as if the flow of goods left a material accounting on the person
performing the service. The record allows the work to be reactivated in derived virtual or
installation formats.</p></section>
