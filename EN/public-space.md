<h1 class="titulo">Public Spaces</h1>
<h2 class="subtitulo">From the series The Plague – Displacement of Painting</h2>
<h3 class="subtitulo2">Urban interventions and pictorial displacement in shared space</h3>

<figure class="imagen-con-caption">
  <img src="/assets/img/desp-publico-bustoohigg-002.jpg" alt="Intervention on a civic monument from The Plague" loading="lazy">
  <figcaption>Saturation / The Plague / Displacement of Painting: Public Spaces; The Civic. Intervention on Bernardo O'Higgins monument, Santiago, Chile, 2010.</figcaption>
</figure>

<p class="parrafo">
  <strong>Public Spaces</strong> gathers works and actions where the displacement of painting enters the common environment: walls, urban furniture, transit surfaces, monuments, and places of encounter. The aim is not to decorate the city, but to produce friction.
</p>

<p class="parrafo">
  Printed paper operates here as expanded painting: a massive, disposable, contemporary material that returns as skin, evidence, and visual pressure in the urban landscape.
</p>
