<h1 class="titulo">Muralism</h1>
<h2 class="subtitulo">Open-air museums</h2>

<figure class="imagen-con-caption">
  <img src="/assets/img/muralismo/muralismo-museo-al-padre-completo.jpg" alt="Large-scale mural in Villa Alberto Larraguibel" loading="eager">
  <figcaption>Large-scale mural documented within a neighborhood open-air museum context.</figcaption>
</figure>

<p class="parrafo" style="margin-top: 6%;">
  In <strong>open-air museums</strong>, muralism is no longer conceived as a single isolated work but as a territorial network of images. The street, the facade, everyday circulation, and neighborhood memory all become part of the reading system.
</p>

<p class="parrafo">
  This modality makes it possible to install projects with broader urban and community scope, where each wall participates in a larger narrative. The work is inscribed in the neighborhood, in dialogue with homes, paths, and local identity, so context is not just background: it is constitutive of the project.
</p>

<p class="parrafo">
  In the archive, this line gathers experiences where muralism is articulated with the idea of route, public memory, and the symbolic construction of territory.
</p>
