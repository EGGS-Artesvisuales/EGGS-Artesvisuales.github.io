<section class="parrafo">
  <p><strong>Musoe Open Air</strong> is a series of mural diptychs painted on the facades of
  three-story residential buildings. The works portray men and women from Mapuche, Aymara, Rapa Nui,
  and Selk'nam cultures.</p>
  <p>The series was completed in La Florida, Santiago, between 2018 and 2021. It comprises four mural
  diptychs, each panel measuring 8.0 × 6.0 meters, made with acrylic enamel and varnish on brick in
  Villa Alberto Larraguibel.</p>
  <p>The name <em>Musoe</em> combines the artist's teenage graffiti name, <em>Jusoe</em>, with the
  Spanish word <em>museo</em>. The diptych format responds to the paired architecture of the site and
  enables complementary readings: man/woman, figure/symbol, presence/territory.</p>
</section>

<h3 class="subtitulo2">MURAL DIPTYCHS (2018–2021)</h3>
<section class="parrafo"><p>Portraiture, historically associated with distinction and honor, is used
here as a strategy of recognition. The images give public scale and visibility to Indigenous
presence, treating the image itself as territory and memory.</p></section>
