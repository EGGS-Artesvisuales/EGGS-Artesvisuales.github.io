<h2 class="subtitulo">Saturation and contamination in the pictorial language</h2>

<hr class="separador separador--aire" />

<section class="parrafo">
  <p><strong>Infected Paintings</strong> explores the saturation and contamination of the contemporary landscape through a hybrid pictorial language. Painting collides with residual materials from consumer culture: plastic caps, packaging, printed paper, industrial PVC, discarded advertising tarps and recycled supports.</p>
  <p>The series treats the pictorial surface as a body affected by external agents. Skin, territory and painting become parallel surfaces: noble or natural fields invaded by industrial remains, advertising fragments and symptoms of ecological degradation.</p>
  <p>The work develops through two main lines: interventions on traditional supports, where waste alters the academic field of painting; and soft or pre-contaminated supports, where the material already carries traces of use, circulation and discard.</p>
  <p>This archive keeps the curatorial structure of the series. For circulation, campaigns and direct purchase of available editions, use the short landing and the shop links below.</p>
</section>
