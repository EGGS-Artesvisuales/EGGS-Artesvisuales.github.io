<section class="parrafo">
  <p><strong>Papelucho</strong> is a 2022 sculpture based on the character from Chilean children's
  literature. The figure holds a pencil and notebook, attributes associated with writing,
  imagination, and everyday experience narrated from childhood.</p>
  <p>The work was commissioned by the Corporación Cultural de La Florida and managed by Mauricio
  Bascuñán. It is the first completed piece in <strong>pugliEGGSe</strong>, a projected sculptural
  line for reinterpreting recognizable characters from popular culture. No other works in that line
  have yet been completed.</p>
</section>

<h3 class="subtitulo2">MEMORY OF THE WORK</h3>
<section class="parrafo">
  <p>The material loss does not close the work. Its documentation reconstructs scale, color, posture,
  and process, preserving the first formulation of the pugliEGGSe line.</p>
  <p><strong>Meme Painting</strong> is another series within Images of the Popular. It remains in
  development and, because it has no completed works, is not presented here as finished production.</p>
</section>
