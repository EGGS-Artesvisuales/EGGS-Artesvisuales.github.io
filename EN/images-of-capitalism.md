<section class="parrafo">
  <p><strong>Images of Capitalism</strong> brings together works that examine how economic systems
  become body, behavior, and image. The series addresses labor and suspension, the personification
  of corporate power, everyday consumption, the social memory of the pandemic, and administered
  violence as a cultural object.</p>
  <p>The works do not illustrate a single theory. Each one builds a concrete situation through
  photography, drawing, action, assemblage, or installation. Together they form a critical sequence
  from the working body to symbols of authority, and from circulating waste to devices that condense
  a political order.</p>
</section>
